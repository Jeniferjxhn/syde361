For urban planning & arch eng what the numbers mean for design

▲ Heat sink flagged Ring Road / Columbia bus stop

Asphalt here has held a +11°C surface differential over the vegetated control through every afternoon this week, and it's still radiating heat 3 hours after sunset. Pedestrians wait here with zero shade. Candidate intervention: shade structure or tree planting on the south edge; high-albedo repaving is an option, but watch reflected load onto the adjacent facade.

▼ Before / after validated M4 site conversion

The M4 build replaced pavement and a demolished pedestrian bridge with a structure plus planted edges. Comparing this July against the pre-construction baseline at the same point:

Before (paved, Jul 2024)
ΔT +9.8°C
After (built + planted)
ΔT +3.1°C

A 6.7°C reduction in peak surface differential — evidence that the siting decision reduced, rather than compounded, the campus heat-island effect. This is the before/after monitoring loop satellite data can't do.

● Performing as designed Ken's Garden bed

The planted bed is delivering steady cooling: surface temps track 6–8°C below adjacent pavement all afternoon. One caution for the plantings themselves — reflected heat off the neighbouring concrete and glass pushes the bed's east edge ~2°C warmer, and this week's humidity is in the range where fungal disease risk rises for the roses. Green infrastructure works here, but it needs the monitoring to stay working.

◆ Watch SLC / QNC courtyard

Concrete plaza runs a +7°C differential at peak with high solar exposure (48k lux) and no midday shade. It's the campus's favourite outdoor sitting area — exactly where students want to be. If any site justifies shade sails or added canopy for comfort-hours gained per dollar, it's this one. Track comfort-hours before and after any change.

What this tool honestly can't tell you: wind exposure (no anemometer — commuters' worst variable), occupancy/busyness, air quality, and winter behaviour (ice, snow load, freeze-thaw). Data shown is July-only; don't extrapolate seasonal claims from it.
